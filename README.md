### HACKATHONS
Hackathons have become one of the most popular events for Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) students. They provide an opportunity to solve real-world problems, improve technical skills, network with industry professionals, and showcase creativity.
Whether you are a beginner or an experienced programmer, participating in hackathons helps you gain practical experience beyond classroom learning.
### What is a Hackathon?
A **hackathon** is a time-bound event where individuals or teams collaborate to design, build, and present a software or hardware project that solves a specific problem.
Hackathons typically last **24–72 hours**, although some online hackathons may run for several weeks.

The word **Hackathon** combines:
*Hack** → Creative problem-solving (not illegal hacking)
- **Marathon** → Continuous work over a limited period

During a hackathon, participants develop applications, websites, AI models, IoT systems, cybersecurity solutions, or other innovative technologies.
### Why are Hackathons Conducted?
Organizations conduct hackathons to:
- Encourage innovation
- Solve real-world problems
- Discover talented developers
- Recruit interns and full-time employees

### What is Reverse Engineering?
Reverse Engineering is the process of analyzing an existing software, hardware, or system to understand how it works without having access to its original design or source code. It involves examining the structure, functionality, and behavior of a product to discover its internal components and working principles.
Reverse engineering is widely used in software development, cybersecurity, malware analysis, hardware design, and compatibility testing. It helps developers and security professionals understand complex systems, identify vulnerabilities, and improve existing technologies.
#### Benefits of Reverse Engineering
-  Understand how existing software or hardware works.
-  Analyze malware and detect security threats.
-  Identify bugs and vulnerabilities in applications.
- Improve software compatibility and interoperability.
- Recover lost or undocumented system designs.
-  Learn programming techniques used in real-world applications.
-  Modernize legacy software systems.
-  Support digital forensics and cybersecurity investigations.
-  Enhance product quality through detailed analysis.
- Encourage innovation by studying existing technologies.
#### Common Applications
- Software Analysis
- Malware & Virus Analysis
- Cybersecurity
- Digital Forensics
- Hardware Design
- Game Modding
## Career Options in Computer Science & Engineering (CSE)
Computer Science & Engineering (CSE) offers a wide range of career opportunities across the technology industry.
